Delivery Tips

  • Timing: Keep lecture to 15-20 minutes maximum. The activity is where real learning happens.
  • Engagement: Ask "Who has used job search websites?" to connect concepts to student experience.
  • Examples: Use familiar Japanese companies (Toyota, Sony, Rakuten) when demonstrating concepts.
  • Transition: Emphasize that the hands-on activity is where students will truly understand these concepts.

Common Questions & Answers

Q: "What if we can't find good data sources?"

A: "Great question! The activity will help you evaluate options, and we'll learn web scraping in Week 4 to collect data ourselves."

Q: "Do we need to be good at programming?"

A: "Not at all! We'll learn what we need as we go. Today, focus on understanding what data is and why quality matters."

Q: "How do we fix data quality issues?"

A: "For now, just identify and understand them. In Weeks 7-9, we'll learn specific techniques for cleaning and preparing data."

After the Lecture

  • Immediately transition students to the hands-on activity
  • Students work with ML-101 Bot guidance (bot handles step-by-step instructions)
  • Circulate to answer complex questions and provide support
  • Encourage students to explore data sources they're genuinely interested in

Assessment Connection

The Week 2 assignment requires students to submit:

  1. Data Type Analysis Document (analyzing 3 sample datasets)
  2. Data Quality Assessment Report (identifying 5+ quality issues)
  3. Data Source Identification Report (5-7 potential sources)
  4. Source Evaluation Matrix (rating sources on 5 criteria)

All deliverables are completed during the in-class activity with bot guidance.
